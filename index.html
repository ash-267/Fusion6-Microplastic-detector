What are Microplastics?

Microplastics are tiny plastic particles less than 5mm in size. They are found in water, soil, and even the air we breathe. How They Enter Water Bodies: 1.Plastic Waste Breakdown: Sunlight, waves, and weather break bigger plastic into smaller fragments. 2.Industrial & Household Sources : Microbeads from cosmetics, fibers from synthetic clothes during washing, tire wear particles. 3.Improper Waste Disposal : Littering and poor waste management allow plastics to flow into rivers, lakes, and oceans. 4.Wastewater Discharge : Treatment plants often fail to filter all microplastics, so they reach water bodies.
